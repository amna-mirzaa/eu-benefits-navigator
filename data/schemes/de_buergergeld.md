# Bürgergeld — Germany

Bürgergeld (formerly Hartz IV / ALG II) is Germany's basic income support for people who are able to work but cannot cover their living costs, whether unemployed or working with low income.

## Eligibility

- Must be at least 15 years old and below the standard statutory retirement age.
- Must live permanently in Germany.
- Must be capable of working at least 3 hours a day (physically, mentally, emotionally).
- Must be in financial need: income and/or assets are not sufficient to cover living expenses, and no sufficient support is available from relatives or other social benefit providers.
- Asset limit: a single adult can hold up to €40,000 in savings/assets without losing eligibility (higher for larger households).
- Foreign nationals can qualify under specific residence/work-authorization conditions — e.g., having been a registered resident in Germany for at least 5 years, or holding a valid work-authorizing residence permit. Official guidance is issued by the Federal Employment Agency (Bundesagentur für Arbeit).
- Recipients of Bürgergeld are generally not simultaneously eligible for Wohngeld, since housing costs are already covered under Bürgergeld.

## How to apply

Apply through the local Jobcenter, in person or online, via the Federal Employment Agency.
