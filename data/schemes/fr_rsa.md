# RSA (Active Solidarity Income) — France

RSA is France's minimum income guarantee for people with little or no income, intended to be topped up by, or combined with, professional activity.

## Eligibility

- Open to people aged 25 or over.
- Also open to younger workers aged 18–24 if they are single parents, or if they have accumulated at least 3,214 hours of paid work (roughly two years) over the preceding three years.
- Must have stable, effective, and permanent residence in metropolitan France or eligible overseas departments — defined as at least nine months' presence per year.
- Must have limited assets and income (means-tested).
- Non-French nationals must be EEA citizens, or hold a residence permit that has been valid for at least five years.
- Since January 2025, all recipients must register with France Travail and sign a reciprocal "engagement contract," typically requiring 15–20 hours/week of job-search, training, or similar integration activities to keep the benefit.

## How to apply

Apply via CAF (caf.fr) or the MSA for agricultural-sector claimants; registration with France Travail is mandatory alongside the claim.
