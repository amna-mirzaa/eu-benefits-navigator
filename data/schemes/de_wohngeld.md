# Wohngeld — Germany

Wohngeld is a subsidy toward rent (Mietzuschuss) or, for owner-occupiers, toward housing costs (Lastenzuschuss), aimed at people with low income who can cover basic living costs but struggle with housing expenses.

## Eligibility

- Available to both renters and property owners with low income relative to their rent/housing costs.
- People who already receive Bürgergeld, full BAföG, or other social assistance that already covers housing costs are generally **excluded** — they get housing support through those benefits instead.
- EU citizens qualify on the same terms as German nationals. Non-EU citizens with a valid residence permit can also apply.
- No single fixed income ceiling: eligibility depends on household size, local rent level (Mietstufe), and income after deductions. As a rough guide, a single person may qualify with gross monthly income up to roughly €1,500–€1,800; a family of four up to roughly €3,500–€4,000, though the maximum band nationally is quoted as €2,061–€5,244 depending on circumstances.
- Applies to both rented and owned homes.

## Amount

Average payout is roughly €370/month, varying by rent, income, and household size.

## How to apply

Apply at the local Wohngeldbehörde (housing benefit office), typically a branch of the local citizens' office (Bürgeramt). Benefit is usually granted for 12 months and must be renewed.
